🔥 Proper Hot Curry — No Apologies Made

Following on from our spice-frying discussion — this recipe uses the sequential frying method (whole spices first, ground spices second) to maximise depth and heat. This is a lamb curry, because lamb can take punishment.

🌿 Spice List

Whole Spices (Stage 1 — fry first)

  • 2 black cardamom pods
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp black mustard seeds
  • 6 whole black peppercorns
  • 2 dried Kashmiri chillies
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 bay leaves

Ground Spices (Stage 2 — add after onions)

  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder (colour + mild heat)
  • 1½ tsp cayenne pepper (this is where the fire lives)
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1½ tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala (held back for the end)
  • ½ tsp black pepper, ground
  • ½ tsp fenugreek powder (methi — adds bitterness and depth)

Fresh Heat

  • 6 green bird's eye chillies, slit lengthways (do not remove seeds)
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated fine
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced

🛒 Full Ingredients

Item Amount
Lamb shoulder, bone-in, cut into chunks 1 kg
Onions, finely diced 3 large
Tomatoes, chopped 3 medium
Tomato paste 2 tbsp
Plain yoghurt 4 tbsp
Oil (mustard oil preferred, or neutral) 4 tbsp
Salt to taste
Water or stock 300–400 ml
Fresh coriander to finish

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Method

Step 1 — Marinate the Lamb (do this first, ideally overnight)

  1. Combine lamb with yoghurt, half the garlic, half the ginger, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp cayenne, and salt.
  2. Mix well, cover, refrigerate for at least 2 hours — overnight is better.
  3. The yoghurt tenderises the meat and the spices penetrate deep.

Step 2 — Bloom the Whole Spices

  1. Heat oil in a heavy-based pot (cast iron or thick-bottomed) over medium-high heat until shimmering.
  2. Add black cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and bay leaves first — 30 seconds, stirring.
  3. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds — wait for the mustard seeds to pop (about 20–30 seconds).
  4. Add green cardamom, peppercorns, and dried Kashmiri chillies — another 30 seconds.
  5. You should smell a deep, toasty, complex aroma. If it smells burnt, you've gone too far — start over.

Each spice gets its moment. This is the sequential method we discussed — you're extracting different compounds at different rates.

Step 3 — Build the Base

  1. Add diced onions to the spiced oil. Cook low and slow — 20–25 minutes until deep golden brown. Not blonde. Not caramel. Dark golden brown. This is non-negotiable.
  2. Add remaining garlic and ginger. Fry for 3–4 minutes until raw smell disappears.
  3. Add bird's eye chillies. Stir through.

Step 4 — Add Ground Spices

  1. Turn heat to medium-low.
  2. Add chilli powder, cayenne, coriander, cumin, turmeric, and black pepper.
  3. Stir constantly for 2–3 minutes — you want the spices frying in the oil, not steaming. Add a splash of water if it sticks.
  4. The paste should darken slightly and the oil will start to separate from the masala — that's your cue to move on.

Step 5 — Add Tomatoes

  1. Add chopped tomatoes and tomato paste.
  2. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, for 10–12 minutes until tomatoes completely break down and the oil separates again.
  3. This is your masala base — it should be thick, dark, and deeply fragrant.

Step 6 — Brown the Lamb

  1. Add marinated lamb pieces directly to the masala.
  2. Turn heat to high and bhunao — stir-fry the lamb in the masala for 8–10 minutes, letting it catch slightly on the bottom before stirring.
  3. This step builds the fond and seals flavour into the meat. Don't rush it.

Step 7 — Slow Cook

  1. Add 300–400 ml water or stock — enough to just cover the meat.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer.
  3. Cover and cook for 60–75 minutes until lamb is tender and falling from the bone.
  4. Check every 20 minutes — stir, scrape the bottom, adjust liquid if needed.

Step 8 — Finish

  1. Remove lid, turn heat to medium, and reduce the gravy to your preferred consistency — 10–15 minutes uncovered.
  2. Stir in garam masala and taste for salt.
  3. Optional: a small knob of butter stirred in at the end adds gloss and rounds the heat.
  4. Finish with fresh coriander.

🌡️ Heat Level Notes

This is genuinely spicy — not "restaurant spicy." Adjust the bird's eye chillies if you want to dial it back. Add more if you enjoy suffering.
