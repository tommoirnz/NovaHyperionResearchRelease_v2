General Relativity & Black Holes

The Core Idea

Einstein's General Relativity (GR) replaces Newton's "gravity as a force" with a far more elegant concept:

Mass and energy curve spacetime. Objects follow the straightest possible paths (geodesics) through that curved spacetime.

What we perceive as gravitational attraction is simply geometry — you're not being pulled toward Earth, you're following a curved path through warped spacetime.

Spacetime Curvature

Spacetime curvature around a massive object — the geometry that replaces "gravitational force"

The Einstein Field Equations

The master equation of GR:

Gμν+Λgμν=8πGc4Tμν

Symbol Meaning
Gμν Einstein tensor — describes spacetime curvature
Λ Cosmological constant (dark energy / expansion of universe)
gμν Metric tensor — encodes the geometry of spacetime
Tμν Stress-energy tensor — encodes mass, energy, momentum
G Newton's gravitational constant
c Speed of light

The left side is geometry. The right side is matter/energy. Beautifully compact — and hideously difficult to solve in general.

The Schwarzschild Solution

Karl Schwarzschild solved the field equations in 1916 (while serving on the Russian front — remarkable) for a non-rotating, spherically symmetric mass in a vacuum.

The Schwarzschild metric describes the spacetime geometry around such a mass:

ds2=−(1−rsr)c2dt2+(1−rsr)−1dr2+r2dΩ2

where:

dΩ2=dθ2+sin2θdϕ2

is the solid angle element, and rs is the Schwarzschild radius.

Breaking Down the Metric

Term Physical Meaning
−(1−rsr)c2dt2 Time dilation — clocks slow near mass
(1−rsr)−1dr2 Radial length contraction near mass
r2dΩ2 Angular part — spherical symmetry

Far from the mass (r≫rs), the metric reduces to flat Minkowski spacetime — as expected.

The Schwarzschild Radius

The critical radius at which the escape velocity equals c:

rs=2GMc2

Object Schwarzschild Radius
Sun rs≈3 km
Earth rs≈9 mm
Stellar black hole (10 M⊙) rs≈30 km

The Event Horizon

The event horizon sits at r=rs — the point of no return. It's not a physical surface; it's a boundary in spacetime beyond which:

escape velocity≥c

No signal, no matter, no information can escape from r<rs.

The proper time for an infalling observer to reach the event horizon is finite:

τ=∫rrsdrrsr−1⋅c

But to a distant observer, the infalling object appears to slow asymptotically and never quite reach rs — time dilation becomes infinite at the horizon.

Gravitational Time Dilation

A clock at radius r runs slower than one at infinity:

dτdt=1−rsr

  • At r=rs: time stops entirely — dτdt=0
  • At r→∞: coordinate time and proper time agree

Geodesic Equation

Free-falling objects follow geodesics — paths that extremise proper time:

d2xμdτ2+Γαβμdxαdτdxβdτ=0

where Γαβμ are the Christoffel symbols — connection coefficients that encode how the coordinate basis vectors change across curved spacetime.

Key Radii for a Schwarzschild Black Hole

Feature Radius Description
Event Horizon r=2GMc2 Point of no return
Photon Sphere r=3GMc2 Unstable circular photon orbit
ISCO r=6GMc2 Innermost stable circular orbit for matter

The ISCO (Innermost Stable Circular Orbit) is where accretion discs terminate — matter inside this radius spirals rapidly into the black hole.

Hawking Radiation

Quantum effects near the event horizon give black holes a temperature:

TH=ℏc38πGMkB

More massive black holes are colder:

  • A stellar black hole has TH∼10−8 K — utterly negligible
  • A microscopic black hole would be searingly hot and evaporate almost instantly

The Kerr Metric (Rotating Black Holes)

Real black holes rotate. The Kerr metric (1963) extends Schwarzschild to include angular momentum J:

ds2=−(1−rsrΣ)c2dt2−2rsrasin2θΣcdtdϕ+ΣΔdr2+Σdθ2+(r2+a2+rsra2sin2θΣ)sin2θdϕ2

where:

a=JMc,Σ=r2+a2cos2θ,Δ=r2−rsr+a2

Rotation introduces frame dragging — spacetime itself is dragged around with the black hole, creating an ergosphere outside the event horizon where nothing can remain stationary.

General Relativity is one of the most beautiful theories in physics — a geometric description of gravity that has survived every experimental test thrown at it for over a century, from Mercury's precessing orbit to gravitational waves detected by LIGO.
