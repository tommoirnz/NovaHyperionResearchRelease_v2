Nuclear Fusion Physics: D-T Reaction & Plasma Confinement

Context for simulation — pure physics, no code

1. The D-T Reaction

The deuterium-tritium reaction is the most accessible fusion reaction for engineering purposes:

12H+13H→24He+01n

Why D-T? It has the highest cross-section at the lowest temperature (~70 keV peak), making it the prime candidate for first-generation fusion reactors.

2. Energy Released Per Reaction

Using mass-energy equivalence E=Δm⋅c2:

  • α-particle (4He): 3.52 MeV (20%) — stays in plasma, heats it
  • Neutron: 14.07 MeV (80%) — escapes plasma, captured in blanket for heat/tritium breeding

3. The Lawson Criterion

John Lawson (1957) derived the minimum condition for a plasma to produce more energy than it consumes — the break-even condition.

Triple Product

The key figure of merit is the fusion triple product:

nTτE≥3×1021 m−3⋅keV⋅s

Where:

  • n = plasma number density (particles/m³)
  • T = plasma temperature (keV)
  • τE = energy confinement time (seconds) — how long the plasma retains its energy

Ignition Condition

For ignition (self-sustaining burn, α-heating exceeds losses):

nτE≥12kBT⟨σv⟩Eα

Where:

  • ⟨σv⟩ = reactivity — thermally averaged fusion cross-section × velocity
  • Eα=3.52 MeV — energy deposited back into plasma
  • kBT — thermal energy of the plasma

At optimal D-T temperature T≈10–20 keV:

nτE≳2×1020 m−3⋅s

4. Plasma Confinement

Fusion plasma must reach T∼108 K — hotter than the Sun's core. No material wall can contain it directly.

4.1 Magnetic Confinement (Tokamak)

Charged particles spiral along magnetic field lines. A tokamak uses a toroidal + poloidal field geometry:

F→=q(v→×B→)

The Larmor radius (gyration radius of a particle):

rL=mv⊥qB

Confinement requires rL≪ plasma radius. For a proton at 10 keV in B=5 T:

rL≈2mpEkeB≈3 mm

The safety factor q governs field line winding and MHD stability:

q=rBϕRBθ

Stability requires q>1 (Kruskal-Shafranov condition).

4.2 Energy Balance in Confinement

Power balance in steady state:

Pα+Paux=Prad+WτE

Where:

  • Pα=14n2⟨σv⟩Eα — alpha heating power density
  • Paux — external heating (neutral beams, RF)
  • Prad — Bremsstrahlung + synchrotron radiation losses
  • W=32nkBT — plasma stored energy

4.3 Bremsstrahlung Radiation Loss

Unavoidable radiative loss from electron-ion collisions:

Pbrem∝n2Z2T1/2

This sets a lower bound on impurity tolerance — high-Z impurities radiate catastrophically.

5. Fusion Cross-Section & Reactivity

The D-T fusion cross-section σ(E) peaks at E≈70 keV (centre-of-mass frame):

σDTmax≈5 barns=5×10−28 m2

The thermally averaged reactivity for a Maxwellian plasma:

⟨σv⟩=∫0∞σ(v)⋅v⋅f(v)dv

Where f(v) is the Maxwell-Boltzmann speed distribution. Peak reactivity for D-T occurs at T≈70 keV, but practical reactor designs target T≈10–20 keV for efficiency.
