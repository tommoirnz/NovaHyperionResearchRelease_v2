🚀 PROPOSAL 1: The Hormuz International Zone

The Problem: Both sides weaponise the Strait of Hormuz as leverage.

The Solution: Internationalise it completely.

  • Establish the Strait of Hormuz International Maritime Authority (SHIMA) — jointly administered by Iran, Gulf states, the EU, China, and India
  • Iran gets guaranteed revenue from transit fees — approximately $4–6 billion annually at realistic toll rates (currently zero)
  • Military vessels from all nations — including the U.S. and Israel — require advance notification and consent
  • A multinational monitoring fleet (not NATO, not U.S.) patrols the strait under UN mandate

Why it works: Iran gets economic dignity and sovereignty recognition. The West gets predictable oil flows. Everyone stops using tankers as chess pieces.

Why it's radical: It requires the U.S. to accept it doesn't own international waters. Hard sell — but so is another war.
