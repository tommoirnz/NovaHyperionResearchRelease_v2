🏛️ Legislative Highlights

FY2026 National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA)

The conferenced FY2026 NDAA includes three UAP-specific provisions representing the most substantive legislative progress in years:

  1. NORAD/Northcom Intercept Briefings — The Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) must now brief Congress on all UAP intercept operations conducted by NORAD and U.S. Northern Command since 2004, including the number, location, nature, procedures, and any data collected.

    "Creating a statutory obligation to brief Congress on these intercepts is a concrete step toward correcting that deficiency." — Jordan Flowers, Disclosure Foundation

  2. Classification Guide Audit — AARO must account for all UAP-related security classification guides. This directly addresses long-standing concerns that the DoD has been systematically overclassifying material — including previously unclassified imagery — in ways inconsistent with executive order standards.

  3. Streamlined Reporting — Eliminates duplicative reporting requirements and improves how federal agencies share UAP data with AARO.

H.R.1187 — UAP Transparency Act (119th Congress)

A dedicated UAP Transparency Act has been introduced in the House during the 119th Congress (2025–2026), pushing for structured public disclosure mechanisms and greater accountability around UAP data held by federal agencies.

Whistleblower Protection Legislation

New legislation under consideration would add UAP-specific whistleblower protections, allowing individuals to report on the use of taxpayer funds for UAP research under existing whistleblower statutes. The bill mirrors legislation proposed in the previous congressional session and is seen as a critical complement to the transparency push.

📢 Congressional Hearings

"Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena: Exposing the Truth"

A joint hearing by the Subcommittee on Cybersecurity, IT, and Government Innovation and the Subcommittee on National Security, the Border, and Foreign Affairs produced some of the most striking on-record congressional testimony to date.

🧭 Conclusions

The trajectory is unmistakable: Congress is systematically and persistently tightening its grip on UAP oversight. The FY2026 NDAA provisions, the UAP Transparency Act (H.R.1187), and the whistleblower protection bills collectively represent the most substantive legislative push since AARO's establishment in 2022.

The constitutional dimension — whether the executive branch is illegally withholding information from Congress — is now being raised explicitly in formal hearings, a significant escalation in both tone and legal framing. The Disclosure Foundation, chaired by Christopher Mellon, appears to be the primary policy-shaping organisation driving the legislative language behind the scenes.

The overarching pattern: UAP disclosure is no longer a fringe issue. It is now a matter of constitutional law, national security oversight, and legislative enforcement.
