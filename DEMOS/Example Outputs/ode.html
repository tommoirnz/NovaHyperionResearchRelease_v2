🔥 First-Order Nonlinear ODEs — The Logistic Equation!

Oh Tom, this is one of my absolute favourite equations in all of applied mathematics — it's elegant, it's nonlinear, it's everywhere in nature, and the solution is just gorgeous. Let's GO! 🚀

📐 The Equation

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = r\,y\!\left(1 - \frac{y}{K}\right)$$

Symbol Meaning
$y(x)$ Population (or any bounded growing quantity)
$r$ Intrinsic growth rate
$K$ Carrying capacity (the ceiling)

🧠 Why Is This Nonlinear?

A linear first-order ODE has the form:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)\,y = Q(x)$$

The logistic equation, when expanded:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = r\,y - \frac{r}{K}\,y^2$$

contains the term $-\dfrac{r}{K}\,y^2$ — a quadratic in $y$. That's the culprit! Linearity requires the dependent variable and its derivatives to appear only to the first power, with no products of $y$ with itself. The $y^2$ term breaks that rule decisively. ⚡

It's also a Bernoulli equation with $n = 2$, which gives us our roadmap to the solution.

✏️ Step-by-Step Analytical Solution

Step 1 — Separate Variables

Rewrite:

$$\frac{dy}{y\!\left(1 - \dfrac{y}{K}\right)} = r\,dx$$

Step 2 — Partial Fraction Decomposition

Decompose the left-hand side. We need:

$$\frac{1}{y\!\left(1 - \dfrac{y}{K}\right)} = \frac{1}{y} + \frac{\dfrac{1}{K}}{1 - \dfrac{y}{K}}$$

Verify: Putting over a common denominator:

$$\frac{1 - \dfrac{y}{K} + \dfrac{y}{K}}{y\!\left(1 - \dfrac{y}{K}\right)} = \frac{1}{y\!\left(1 - \dfrac{y}{K}\right)} \checkmark$$

So:

$$\left(\frac{1}{y} + \frac{1/K}{1 - y/K}\right)dy = r\,dx$$

Step 3 — Integrate Both Sides

$$\int \frac{1}{y}\,dy + \int \frac{1/K}{1 - y/K}\,dy = \int r\,dx$$

For the second integral, let $u = 1 - y/K$, so $du = -dy/K$, giving $dy = -K\,du$:

$$\int \frac{1/K}{u}(-K\,du) = -\int \frac{du}{u} = -\ln|u|$$

Therefore:

$$\ln|y| - \ln\!\left|1 - \frac{y}{K}\right| = rx + C_0$$

Step 4 — Combine Logarithms

$$\ln\!\left|\frac{y}{1 - \dfrac{y}{K}}\right| = rx + C_0$$

Exponentiate both sides:

$$\frac{y}{1 - \dfrac{y}{K}} = A\,e^{rx}, \qquad A = e^{C_0}$$

Step 5 — Apply Initial Condition $y(0) = y_0$

At $x = 0$:

$$\frac{y_0}{1 - \dfrac{y_0}{K}} = A$$

So:

$$A = \frac{y_0}{1 - \dfrac{y_0}{K}} = \frac{K\,y_0}{K - y_0}$$

Step 6 — Solve for $y$

From:

$$\frac{y}{1 - \dfrac{y}{K}} = A\,e^{rx}$$

$$y = A\,e^{rx}\!\left(1 - \frac{y}{K}\right) = A\,e^{rx} - \frac{A\,e^{rx}}{K}\,y$$

$$y\!\left(1 + \frac{A\,e^{rx}}{K}\right) = A\,e^{rx}$$

$$\boxed{y(x) = \frac{K\,y_0}{y_0 + (K - y_0)\,e^{-rx}}}$$

✅ Verification — Substituting Back

Let me define for clarity:

$$D(x) = y_0 + (K - y_0)\,e^{-rx}$$

so $y = \dfrac{K\,y_0}{D}$.

Compute $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{-K\,y_0\,D'}{D^2}, \qquad D' = -r(K - y_0)\,e^{-rx}$$

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{K\,y_0\,r(K - y_0)\,e^{-rx}}{D^2}$$

Compute $r\,y\!\left(1 - \dfrac{y}{K}\right)$:

$$1 - \frac{y}{K} = 1 - \frac{y_0}{D} = \frac{D - y_0}{D} = \frac{(K-y_0)\,e^{-rx}}{D}$$

$$r\,y\!\left(1 - \frac{y}{K}\right) = r \cdot \frac{K\,y_0}{D} \cdot \frac{(K - y_0)\,e^{-rx}}{D} = \frac{r\,K\,y_0\,(K-y_0)\,e^{-rx}}{D^2}$$

Both sides are identical ✅ 🎉

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = r\,y\!\left(1 - \frac{y}{K}\right) \checkmark$$

🌊 The S-Curve (Sigmoidal) Behaviour

The solution $y(x) = \dfrac{K\,y_0}{y_0 + (K - y_0)\,e^{-rx}}$ has three beautiful regimes:

Regime Behaviour
$y \ll K$ Exponential growth $\approx y_0\,e^{rx}$
$y \approx K/2$ Maximum growth rate (inflection point)
$y \to K$ Growth saturates — asymptote at $K$

The inflection point occurs exactly at $y = K/2$, which you can verify by computing $\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 0$. 🔍
