Famous Unsolved Problems in Mathematics 🧮

Alright, let's tour the mathematical frontier — problems that have resisted the collective brainpower of humanity, some for centuries.

The Millennium Prize Problems ($1M each)

The Clay Mathematics Institute posted these in 2000. Six remain unsolved:

Problem Area The Gist
Riemann Hypothesis Number Theory All non-trivial zeros of the zeta function have real part ½
P versus NP Computer Science Can every problem whose solution is quickly verified also be quickly solved?
Navier–Stokes Existence & Smoothness PDEs/Physics Do smooth solutions always exist for the equations governing fluid flow?
Hodge Conjecture Algebraic Geometry Certain cohomology classes on projective varieties are algebraic
Birch & Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture Number Theory Relates the rank of an elliptic curve to the behaviour of its L-function at s=1
Yang–Mills Existence & Mass Gap Mathematical Physics Prove quantum Yang–Mills theory exists and has a positive mass gap

The seventh — the Poincaré Conjecture — was solved by Grigori Perelman in 2003 (who famously declined both the prize and the Fields Medal). Though notably, the smooth 4D Poincaré conjecture remains open.

Other Legendary Open Problems

Number Theory

  • Goldbach's Conjecture — Every even integer > 2 is the sum of two primes. Tested to absurd limits, never proved.
  • Twin Prime Conjecture — Infinitely many primes $p$ where $p+2$ is also prime.
  • Landau's Problems (1912) — All four still open after 113 years!

Algebra & Geometry

  • Casas-Alvero Conjecture — A polynomial sharing a root with each of its derivatives must be a perfect power of a linear factor.
  • Eilenberg–Ganea Conjecture — Can a group with cohomological dimension 2 always have a 2D classifying space?

Combinatorics & Logic

  • Erdős's Problems — Over 1,212 problems posed across six decades; 703 remain unsolved.

Recent Progress (2024–2025)

Some notable resolutions:

  • Eremenko's Conjecture (2025) — Proved by David Martí-Pete, Lasse Rempe, and James Waterman: every component of the escaping set of an entire transcendental function is unbounded. ✅
  • Zimmer's Conjecture — Resolved by Aaron Brown, David Fisher, and Sebastián Hurtado-Salazar.
  • Einstein Problem (aperiodic monotile) — Solved by David Smith et al. with the "hat" tile.

The Vibe

What I find genuinely fascinating is the distribution of difficulty. Some of these problems can be stated to a bright 12-year-old (Goldbach, Twin Primes), yet the proofs — if they exist — seem to require mathematics that may not have been invented yet.

And Erdős alone left behind 703+ open problems. The man was basically a mathematical Johnny Appleseed, scattering conjectures across the landscape for future generations to harvest.
